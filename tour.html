Starting to work with an interior design company can be an exciting and rewarding experience.
If you're interested in working with an interior design company but don't know where to start,
here's a simple guide to get you going:

Determine your goals and expectations: Before you start looking for an interior design company,
take some time to think about what you want to achieve with your space.
What kind of look and feel do you want? What are your goals for the space?
What are your expectations from the interior design company?
Create a list to keep track
Todo Page

Do your research: Once you have a clear idea of what you want to achieve,
start researching interior design companies that fit your style and budget.
Look for reviews and portfolios to get a sense of their work and reputation.
Find One Here
Gallery
Schedule a consultation: Once you have narrowed down your choices,
schedule a consultation with the interior design company.
During the consultation, you can discuss your goals and expectations,
and get a sense of the designer's communication style and approach to design.
You can get a rough view by visiting their website
Set a budget: It's important to set a budget before starting any interior design project.
Discuss your budget with the interior design company and make sure they can work within your budget.
Try to get documents ready like your floor plans and 360 images
Select materials and furnishings: Once the design plan is in place, work with the interior
design company to select materials and furnishings that fit your style and budget.
We have an experience center with many different finishes and designs, visit us here: Showroom
Execute the plan: Once everything is agreed upon, the interior design company will execute the plan, bringing your vision to life. This will be done through constant updates via 360 photos and life feed

Review and make changes: After the work is complete, review the project and provide feedback to the interior design company.
If there are any changes you would like to make, discuss them with the designer.
